## Risk Register

### Risk 1 — Brand not specified in listing data

**Severity:** HIGH  
**Likelihood:** HIGH  

A vague "vintage designer" label could mean anything from Coach to Chanel. The ROI and authenticity risk are entirely brand-dependent. A mid-tier brand at £180 asking with £360 resale is credible; a Chanel bag at £180 should raise counterfeit flags.

**Mitigation:**
1. Request brand identification from seller before purchase.
2. Cross-reference serial number against brand authentication databases.
3. If brand is counterfeit-sensitive (e.g., Chanel, Louis Vuitton, Gucci at suspiciously low prices), escalate to founder for review.

---

### Risk 2 — Condition not fully disclosed

**Severity:** MEDIUM  
**Likelihood:** MEDIUM  

Vintage items commonly have undisclosed wear: corner scuffing, leather drying, hardware pitting, interior staining. £360 resale depends on accurate condition representation.

**Mitigation:**
1. Request 6+ detailed condition photos (4 corners + interior + hardware).
2. Use Vinted's £10 Item Verification service at point of purchase.
3. Factor a £10–£20 refund reserve into profit calculations for minor undisclosed flaws.
4. Retain all seller photos and messages as evidence for dispute resolution.

---

### Risk 3 — Seller history not specific to luxury bags

**Severity:** MEDIUM  
**Likelihood:** LOW–MEDIUM  

The "3/3" seller history is positive but not confirmed as luxury bag sales specifically. A seller with 3/3 on low-value fashion items carries different risk than one with 3/3 on luxury bags.

**Mitigation:**
1. Ask seller directly: "Do you have any reviews from previous luxury bag sales?"
2. Check seller's Vinted profile for other listed/designer items.
3. If seller has no history with luxury goods, treat as elevated risk and require Vinted Item Verification.
4. Retain all messaging as part of the proof pack.
